usability problems

I have frequented this website as both a reader and editor, and have found the interface to be unintuitive on both fronts. I love The Indy and hope to better highlight the high quality work the paper produces.


i made observations about the usability in a few distinct categories:

Usability Efficiency Learnability Memorability
The ability to navigate by “section” is hidden by a dropdown menu There is no simple table of contents: in order to see all of the issue, one must scroll through the entire site It is slightly difficult to understand how particular parts of the box bring people to an article There is no way to store individual articles or bookmark to read later would could be a useful tool
It is also difficult to distinguish what is being clicked on because the individual sections do not have buttons There is redundancy in the dropdown menu Some links (apply, subscribe, alumni, donate) are dead While navigation is standardized, there is no way to really return to where the user last came from

using WebAIM WAVE, I found the following accessibility issues:

  • Redundant links: when clicking on an article, the whole box and text itself links to new pages
  • Empty links: some of the links don’t go anywhere
  • The all white background and boxes make it slightly difficult to distinguish the articles from the webpage

I agree with the accessibility problems; a lot of the website has unintuitive linking and while that is frustrating as a user, I understand that can also pose accessibility issues for those relying on particular means of moving through the multiple pages.
